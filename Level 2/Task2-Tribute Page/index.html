About the Kim Tae-hyung

Kim Tae-hyung, also known by his stage name V, is a South Korean singer, songwriter, actor, and member of the South Korean boy band BTS (Bangtan Sonyeondan). He was born on December 30, 1995 in Daegu, South Korea and grew up with his family in the city of Geochang. V began his career as a trainee for Big Hit Entertainment, the company that manages BTS, in 2011. He trained for two years before debuting with BTS in 2013. He is known for his unique vocal style, which has been praised by both fans and critics alike. He has also written and produced several songs for the group, and has been credited as a co-writer on many of BTS's biggest hits. As a member of BTS, V has helped the group become one of the most popular and successful boy bands in the world. BTS has won multiple awards and accolades, including several Billboard Music Awards, a Grammy nomination, and multiple awards at the Mnet Asian Music Awards. BTS's music is known for its meaningful and socially conscious lyrics, which often address issues such as mental health and self-empowerment. In addition to his work with BTS, V has also acted in several South Korean dramas and films. He made his acting debut in 2016 with a small role in the drama "Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth." He has also appeared in the drama "Itaewon Class" (2020) and the film "The Divine Fury" (2019). He is known for his natural acting skills and has received positive reviews for his performances.
